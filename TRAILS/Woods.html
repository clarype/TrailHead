Backpacking the Northwest

Hike to Lake of the Woods

Katie and I decided to brave it and head out on a early spring backpacking trip to The Lake of The Woods. I think it was Finn's first backpacking. We started out from the same trailhead as the Jorn Lake trip, but headed northeast by Marion Lake. The round trip distance was about 16.5 kilometers, we strayed from the ture trail due to snowpack. The image above shows the frosty Lake of The Woods.

The Start

leaving the trailhead to The Lake of the Woods. The trails were clear as far as we could see but that would change. About 1.2 kilometers in we came to the first swichtback that brings us up to Lake Ann. We came across several other hikers with fishing poles and other day hikers.

Marion Lake and Jack

Once we got to Marion lake the sight of snow was a litte toubling as I looked up at the beanch above Marion lake. It look like we were going to have to trek over the snowpack to each the lake, which is fine and even makes some aspects of the hike more fun, but there are add dangers.

Finn Lake

Once we got to Finn's lakes an unmarked pooling of snow melt the snowpack was more or less continues. From here on up to the lake contact with the trail intermitent. It seemed like dry feet would be a hard quailty to mantain.

John Swallow's Grave

As I was planning this trip I found that we would pass by a grave of John Swallow. The information I found about John Swallow grave was limited to geograhpic location. At this location the trail split off to the rigth and would take you to the Pacific Creast Trail. We stayed rigth and continued on.

Trail Blazing

As we continued to trail blaze our own path we came across several danger of the snowpack, tree wells, downtrees under the snow and unseen creeks. All of these can melt the snow away from the soil first leaving thin crust of snow for you to step throuh.

Lake of The Woods

We made it to The Lake of The Woods with wet feet, but that always a possiablity. Finn was super tired, but had enough energy to go beary sticks and romp around the snow somemore.
